Tulay Tsambardas — Personal Brand Guidelines

Digital Designer · Melbourne

1. Brand Foundation

Who: Tulay Tsambardas — graphic designer with 14 years of experience, now also a web developer (Certificate IV in Web Development, ICT40120).

Brand idea: "Designer who builds. Developer who designs." The TT brand combines classic editorial craft (serif letterforms, warm paper tones) with clean, accessible web engineering.

Personality: warm, precise, honest, encouraging. The brand speaks in plain language and never hides behind jargon.

Purpose of this document: keep every page, project and social profile visually consistent, and demonstrate professional brand-system thinking to clients and employers.

2. Logo System

The logo is a TT monogram — the initials of Tulay Tsambardas — built from a hand-refined serif "T" drawn in Adobe Illustrator. Two overlapping Ts represent design and development working together: the front T in Deep Ocean Blue, the echo T in Warm Terracotta.

On light backgrounds
On dark backgrounds

Clear space & minimum size

Keep clear space around the monogram equal to the height of the T crossbar. Minimum display size: 32 px height on screen. Do not stretch, rotate, recolour, add effects, or place on busy photographs.

Accessibility note (WCAG 2.2)

Deep Ocean Blue on Cream passes AA and AAA for normal text. Warm Terracotta is decorative: on white or cream it does not reach the 4.5:1 contrast needed for body text — use it for large headings, borders, icons and backgrounds only, never for small text.

4. Typography

Headings — classic serif

Georgia / Times New Roman / Mongolian Baiti

Serif faces carry the editorial, crafted feeling of the brand. Georgia is the web-safe first choice; Times New Roman and Mongolian Baiti are approved alternatives for print and documents.

Body — clean sans-serif

Arial / Helvetica Neue for paragraphs, forms and interfaces. Body size 16 px minimum, line-height 1.6.

5. Usage Rules — Do and Don't

DoDon't
Use Deep Ocean Blue for all primary text elements and buttonsDon't use Terracotta for small or body text (contrast fails)
Keep generous cream space around contentDon't place the logo on photos or patterned backgrounds
Pair one serif heading with sans-serif body textDon't mix more than two type families on one page
Label every fictional project clearly as fictionalDon't use real client or brand names in demo projects
Check contrast (target WCAG 2.2 AA) before publishingDon't rely on colour alone to communicate meaning
